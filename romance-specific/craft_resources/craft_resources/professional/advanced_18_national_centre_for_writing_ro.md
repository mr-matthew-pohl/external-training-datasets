Love stories come in all forms—which one speaks to you?
In uncertain times, we could all use a little extra romance, a touch of humour and a cosy escape from reality. This month, indulge in a mood-boosting read with guidance from multi-bestselling novelist and NCW tutor Rachel Hore, who shares her favourite romance novels.
Rachel is also a tutor on our 12-week How to Write Romantic Fiction course. If you’re dreaming of writing your own love story, find out how she can help you bring it to life here.
Outlander by Diana Gabaldon (Penguin)
Outlander is a historical fantasy novel by American writer Diana Gabaldon, first published in 1991.
1946, and Claire Randall goes to the Scottish Highlands with her husband Frank. It’s a second honeymoon, a chance to re-establish their loving marriage. But one afternoon, Claire walks though a circle of standing stones and vanishes into 1743, where the first person she meets is a British army officer — her husband’s six-times great-grandfather. Find out more
‘Scotland’s answer to Game of Thrones’ — The Herald
Queenie by Candice Carty-Williams (Orion)
Queenie is an adult novel written by British author Candice Carty-Williams, published in 2019.
Meet Queenie: Journalist. Catastrophist. Lonely. Loved. Relatable.
Queenie is in a spiral. As she careens from one questionable decision to another, she finds herself wondering, “What are you doing? Why are you doing it? Who do you want to be?” — all of the quandaries today’s woman must face in a world trying to answer them for her. Find out more
‘A black Bridget Jones’ — Candice Carty-Williams, Queenie
‘Filthy, funny and profound’ — Sharlene Teo, Ponti
In Memoriam by Alice Winn (Penguin)
In Memoriam is a historical LGBTQ+ romance novel by Irish-American writer Alice Winn, published in 2023.
Tracing the love story between two WWI soldiers, In Memoriam is a luminous, heartbreaking meditation on the tragedies of war and the precious sanctuary that can be found in deep human connection. Find out more
‘Birdsong for a new generation’ — Joanna Quinn, The Whalebone Theatre
‘A tender, affecting debut . . . Winn strikingly evokes the torment and brutality of life of the front’ — The Times
Fourth Wing by Rebecca Yarros (Piatkus)
Fourth Wing is a 2023 new adult, high fantasy romance, or ‘romantasy’ novel by American author Rebecca Yarros. It is the first book in the Empyrean series.
Friends, enemies, lovers… Everyone has an agenda.
Violet Sorrengail expected to live a quiet life surrounded by books, until she was forced onto the world’s deadliest training ground. Now she must fight to join the army’s elite: dragon riders. But dragons don’t choose fragile riders, they incinerate them, and when your body breaks as easily as Violet’s does — death is only a heartbeat away. Find out more
Book Lovers by Emily Henry (Penguin)
Book Lovers is a 2022 contemporary romantic comedy novel by American writer Emily Henry.
One summer. Two rivals. A plot twist they didn’t see coming.
Nora is a cut-throat literary agent at the top of her game. Her whole life is books.
Charlie is an editor with a gift for creating bestsellers. And he’s Nora’s work nemesis.
On a getaway trip to Sunshine Falls, a small town straight out of a romance novel, Nora unexpectedly runs into Charlie. Find out more
‘The most phenomenal portrayal of enemies to lovers I have ever read’ — Laura Jane Williams, Our Stop

Maybe Next Time by Cesca Major (HarperCollins)
Maybe Next Time is a magical romantic fiction novel by novelist and screenwriter Cesca Major, published in 2023.
One Day meets Groundhog Day
It is an ordinary Monday and harried London literary agent Emma is flying out of the day as usual. Preoccupied with work and her ever-growing list, she fails to notice her lovely husband Dan seems bereft, her son can barely meet her eye, and her daughter won’t go near here. Even the dog seems sad.
Her whole day is frantic—what else is new—and as she rushes back through the door for dinner, Dan is still upset. They fight, and he walks out, desolate, dragging their poor dog around the block. Just as she realizes it is their anniversary and she has forgotten, again, she hears the screech of brakes. Find out more
You Are Here by David Nicholls (Hodder & Stoughton)
You Are Here is a romance novel by British writer David Nicholls, published in 2024.
Marnie is stuck. Michael is coming undone. But can they survive the journey?
Marnie is tired of being alone and Michael is newly single, both in need of something to get them out of their slump.
When a persistent mutual friend and some very English weather conspire to bring them together, Marnie and Michael suddenly find themselves alone on the most epic of walks and on the precipice of a new friendship. Find out more
You Had Me at Hello by Mhairi Mcfarlane (HarperCollins)
You Had Me at Hello is a romantic comedy novel by Scottish writer Mhairi Mcfarlane, published in 2012.
What happens when the one that got away comes back?
Rachel and Ben. Ben and Rachel. It was them against the world. Until it all fell apart. It’s been a decade since they last spoke, but when Rachel bumps into Ben one rainy day, the years melt away.
They’d been partners in crime and the best of friends. But life has moved on: Ben is married. Rachel is not. Yet in that split second, Rachel feels the old friendship return. And along with it, the broken heart she’s never been able to mend. Find out more
The Song of Achilles by Madeline Miller (Bloomsbury)
The Song of Achilles is a historical LGBTQ+ romance novel by American writer Madeline Miller, published in 2011.
Greece in the age of heroes. Awkward young prince Patroclus has been exiled to the court of King Peleus. Despite their differences, Peleus’ golden boy Achilles befriends the shamed prince. As they grow into young men, their bond blossoms into something deeper.
The Song of Achilles is a queer retelling of the Greek myth of Achilles and Patroclus, the kidnapping of Helen, and the Trojan war. Find out more
‘Sexy, dangerous, mystical’ — Bettany Hughes, historian
First Lie Wins by Ashley Elston (Headline)
First Lie Wins is a psychological thriller crime novel by American novelist Ashely Elston.
There’s an old saying: The first lie wins…
At a party with her fiancé Ryan Sumner, Evie Porter is not looking for trouble. But trouble has found her.
A woman approaches. She’s using her name. And Evie knows it’s a warning.
Because Evie Porter is not her real name. It’s her job.
Ryan Sumner is not her future husband. He’s her target.
And this woman showing up now can only mean one thing. Her employer is watching. One mistake and she’s dead.
To save herself, and escape her employer for good, Evie must set the last perfect trap. Which means using herself as bait…
She’s stolen your identity. Now you have to steal it back. Find out more
‘Buckle up…the best cat and mouse suspense I’ve read in years’ — Lisa Gardner
Serial Romantic: how to write exciting romantic fiction
In this article, bestselling romance author Cressida McLaughlin shares her top tips for writing exciting and experimental romantic fiction.
“I have loved reading romantic books for as long as I can remember, and for the last decade, I’ve been writing them too. It all stems from the one central premise that I can’t get enough of: Will they, or won’t they? Except that with almost all books that can be categorised as romance, the real question is: How will they?”

The Idea of You by Robinne Lee (Penguin)
The Idea of You is a romance novel by American writer Robinne Lee.
Everyone in the world knows his name. But it’s you he wants.
It is a journey that spans continents as Solène and Hayes navigate each other’s worlds: from stadium tours to international art fairs to secluded hideaways.
Exploring rampant sexism, single motherhood and extreme celebrity, a secret relationship blossoms. Find out more
Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow by Gabrielle Zevin (Penguin)
Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow is an adventure novel by American author Gabrille Zevin, published in 2022.
This is the story of Sam and Sadie. It’s not a romance, but it is about love.
Two kids meet in a hospital gaming room in 1987. One is visiting her sister, the other is recovering from a car crash. The days and months are long there. Their love of video games becomes a shared world – of joy, escape and fierce competition. But all too soon that time is over, fades from view. Find out more
The Bookshop on the Shore by Jenny Colgan (Sphere)
The Bookshop on the Shore is a feel-good, cosy romance novel by Scottish writer Jenny Colgan, published in 2019.
In the Scottish Highlands, a tiny bookshop perches on the edge of a loch…
Zoe is a single mother, sinking beneath the waves trying to cope by herself in London. Hari, her gorgeous little boy is perfect in every way – except for the fact that he just doesn’t speak, at all. When her landlord raises the rent on her flat, Zoe doesn’t know where to turn. Find out more
Hold Back the Stars by Katie Khan (Penguin)
Hold Back the Stars is a science fiction romance novel by British writer Katie Kahn.
It is a classic story of boy meets girl. Except that it’s not… The clock is ticking.
Carys and Max only have ninety minutes of air left, looking back on past decisions and their consequences.
A forbidden romance and two people trapped in space, running out of oxygen. Set in on a utopian Earth, the book challenges morality, survival, family and individualism. Citizens are ‘rotated’ every three years to foster growth and no-one can fall in love until the age of thirty-five. Find out more
Ex-Factor by Eva Woods (Claire McGowan) (HarperCollins)
Ex-Factor is a romantic novel written by Northern Irish writer Claire McGowan (penname Eva Woods).
Is it possible to freecycle love?
The story follows Marnie and her three friends, perennially single Helen, recently divorced Rosa and cynical lawyer Ani, as they navigate the world of dating.
Through bad dates and good, four friends begin to realise that there are advantages to dating pre-screen men…but there can be some serious pitfalls to falling for your friend’s ex. Find out more
Honourable mentions
My personal favourites include ‘Coming Home’ by Rosamund Pilcher, ‘The Notebook’ by Nicholas Sparks, ‘The Women’ by Kristin Hannah and ‘The Darkest Hour’ by Barbara Erskine.

Rachel Hore
Rachel Hore is the Sunday Times bestselling author of thirteen novels, including A Place of Secrets, Last Letter Home and A Beautiful Spy, which were all Richard & Judy Book Club picks.  The Glasspainter’s Daughter was shortlisted for the Romantic Novelists Association’s Romantic Novel of the Year and A Gathering Storm for their Historical Novel of the Year category.  Her books have sold more than a million copies and have been widely translated.  They are all at least in part historical, and Norfolk and Suffolk are favourite settings.
After studying Modern History at Oxford University, Rachel initially followed a career in book publishing, working latterly as a Senior Commissioning Editor in Fiction for HarperCollins UK.  Moving with her family to Norwich in 2001, she began to write and her first novel, The Dream House, was published in 2006. She became a lecturer in Publishing and Creative Writing at the University of East Anglia where she taught for many years, leaving just before the Pandemic in 2020.  She currently lives in a rambling Edwardian house in Norwich with her husband, the writer D.J. Taylor, and a fox-red Labrador named Zelda.
Her latest novel, The Hidden Years, set in wartime and 1960s Cornwall, was published in paperback in 2024.  Her next novel, The Secrets of Dragonfly Lodge, will be published in July 2025.
